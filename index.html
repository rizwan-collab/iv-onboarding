Module I

Welcome, Identity, and the Spirit of Khidma

"My Jamat has a long-standing tradition of offering voluntary service. This is a noble tradition and one for which I have the greatest admiration… Our tradition of service is a source of strength and security and hallmark of a community with strong moral and ethical compass."

— Mawlana Hazar Imam, Diwan of the Ismaili Imamat, February 11, 2025

Mission Statement

Drawing inspiration from the teachings of Prophet Muhammad and Hazrat Ali (Peace be upon them) that service to others is an expression of faith, Ismaili Volunteers reflect the Ismaili Jamat's notion of voluntary service as an aspect of devotion that strengthens the community while also cultivating volunteers' self-knowledge and personal development.

Vision Statement — Five Pillars

01
Provide efficient operational services to every Jamatkhana
02
Provide logistical support for all Imamat Institutions
03
Empower youth to develop appreciation for voluntary service
04
Promote and engage in outreach services
05
Provide training to develop core competency skills

Service Areas, Roles, and Organizational Structure

The IV team plays a crucial role in maintaining a serene and spiritual atmosphere within the Jamatkhana. Their responsibilities encompass overseeing various operational aspects, including the kitchen, prayer hall, Nandi area, and lobby.

Safety & Security — Unified Team Structure

The Safety & Security team is a unified team formed by merging the former Safety, Crisis Management (CMV), and Community Safety Lead (CSL) teams. This consolidation enables streamlined internal and external Jamatkhana safety operations, faster emergency response, and a unified strategy for protecting both the Jamatkhana and the Jamat. All former CMV and CSL roles now operate under a single, synchronized S&S leadership.

Module III

Uniform Standards

All Ismaili Volunteers are required to maintain uniform standards at all times while on JK premises or at institutional events. The new uniform reflects the updated IV brand identity.

Men's Western Formal
  • Plain navy blue suit (single breast)
  • Plain white button-down shirt
  • Volunteer Tie
  • Khidma Badge (Issued)
  • Name + Role Badge (left side)
Women's Western Formal
  • Plain navy blue suit
  • Navy pants/skirt (min. knee length)
  • Plain white shirt/blouse
  • Volunteer Scarf or Tie
  • Khidma Badge (Issued)
Women's Eastern Formal
  • Plain navy kurta top
  • Plain white shalwar pant
  • New Volunteer Dupatta
  • Khidma Badge (Issued)

Polo Uniform Policy

On Polos: The IV logo is already embroidered — the Khidma Badge is not worn with the polo. Name/role badge only, on either left or right side. Permitted pant colors: navy, black, or gray (no patterns). Jeans are permitted for youth volunteers only (black, no rips, no patterns). No shorts.

Badge Placement

Name and Title Badges are worn on the left side under the IV Badge on all formal uniforms (western and eastern). On Polos, name badge only — no IV Badge required.

Module IV

Code of Conduct and Infraction Policy

The Code of Conduct sets clear standards, promotes accountability, and ensures ethical, respectful, and responsible behavior in all aspects of service. All Ismaili Volunteers are required to sign, adhere to, and uphold this Code of Conduct as a condition of service.

Purpose of the Code of Conduct

1. Enhance Safety & Security
Protect the well-being of all Jamati members, volunteers, and any individuals the IV interacts with
2. Encourage Growth & Development
Help volunteers grow through cultivating humility, compassion, brotherhood, and strong leadership
3. Adapt to Growth & Change
Address the evolving needs of the IV organization while respecting the Jamat's legacy and tradition
4. Strengthen Teamwork & Collaboration
Foster trust and cooperation among all volunteers, creating mutual respect and harmony
5. Commit to Professionalism & Excellence
Uphold the values of our faith — accountability, ethical behavior, and integrity in all service
6. Set Clear Expectations
Outline what is expected from all volunteers, reducing misunderstandings and ensuring consistency
7. Foster Inclusion
Create a safe, inclusive, respectful space reflecting the pluralism in the Jamat and our communities
8. Provide Conflict Resolution Framework
Establish a basis for addressing misconduct, misunderstandings, or conflicts fairly and consistently
Scope: The Code of Conduct applies on Jamatkhana premises or at institutional events whenever a volunteer is serving operationally — whether or not they are in uniform.

Guiding Principles of the Code of Conduct

Faith & Values
Uphold the ethical principles and values of our faith in every aspect of service
Respect & Dignity
Treat every Jamati member and fellow volunteer with respect, compassion, and dignity
Accountability
Take responsibility for your actions with humility, reflection, and truthfulness
Confidentiality
Protect sensitive information about Jamati members and institutional operations
Unity & Collaboration
Work together with fellow volunteers in a spirit of cooperation, harmony, and brotherhood
Inclusion & Pluralism
Embrace the diversity of the Jamat and the wider communities in which we live and serve

Key Operational Expectations

1. Commitment to Roles & Responsibilities
Volunteers are expected to demonstrate dedication and reliability in their assigned duties.
2. Accountable for Personal Actions, Behavior & Conflict Resolution
Volunteers are expected to take responsibility for their actions and any mistakes with humility, reflection, and truthfulness. Any disputes or conflicts should be resolved through respectful dialogue, communication, or mediation.
3. Professionalism, Integrity & Excellence in Service
All volunteers are expected to uphold the values of our faith by ensuring confidentiality and ethical, respectful, fair, and responsible behavior in all aspects of service.
4. Adherence to Uniform Guidelines
Volunteers must follow ALL uniform policies while serving on the Jamatkhana premises or at Jamati events — including badge placement, approved attire, and the polo vs. formal uniform rules.
5. Following All Policies, SOPs & Local Laws
Volunteers must abide by all Ismaili Volunteer policies, Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), and local laws — particularly those related to safety, security, and Jamatkhana or event operations.

Infraction Classification

⚠️ General 🟠 Medium 🔴 Major 🚫 Critical
Unexcused absences, uniform violations, minor procedural non-compliance Insubordination, disorderly conduct, breach of confidentiality, misuse of authority Serving under influence, weapons on premises, physical altercation, theft, bullying Physical assault, sexual harassment, threats to JK premises or Jamat safety
Coaching, verbal warning, duty reallocation Written warning, 4-week suspension, ethical coaching 8-week suspension, mandatory reflection, possible termination Immediate dismissal, permanent termination

Disciplinary Process

Step 1Report misconduct to appropriate IV leadership (Team Lead, Facilitator, or Director)
Step 2Inquiry and documentation — all infractions investigated in good faith
Step 3Consequences applied based on severity of infraction
Step 4Appeals — Regional or National Leadership within set timeframe
Required Signatures: Code of Conduct Acknowledgement Form + Ismaili Volunteer Waiver — both signed via the electronic Machform link provided by your local JK leadership.

Module V

Safety Awareness and Empathy in Action

Safety is everyone's responsibility. Every Ismaili Volunteer plays an important role in maintaining a safe, respectful, and supportive environment for all Jamati Members and fellow volunteers.

Medical Emergency Protocol

RecognizeStay calm. Identify signs of emergency (heart attack, stroke, allergic reaction)
Contact S&SReach Safety & Security volunteers — they will assist with Health Board professionals
SupportHelp clear the area and provide escort support as needed
DocumentS&S completes incident report immediately
911Never hesitate to dial 911 during an emergency, especially when alone

The OADA Framework for Situational Awareness

O
Observe
Take in the people, things, and movements around you at all times
A
Assess
Does what you see fit the environment? Do they belong here?
D
Decide
Is something wrong? Trust your instincts — if it doesn't feel right, act
A
Act
What do I do? Who do I tell? Inform the appropriate person immediately

Empathy in Action

Empathy is about understanding and sharing others' feelings, even if you haven't experienced the same thing. By actively listening, validating emotions, offering support, and avoiding assumptions, you help create a more compassionate and supportive Jamatkhana community.

⭐ Best Practices for Volunteer Onboarding Programs

Platform design and program management recommendations
🎯
Set a Hard Deadline — Not a Soft Suggestion
Programs with a clear 30-day onboarding window have 2.3x higher completion rates than open-ended ones. Display the deadline prominently on the volunteer dashboard and send automated day-7 and day-14 reminders.
📊
Track Module Drop-off, Not Just Final Completion
If Module III (Uniform) or Module IV (Code of Conduct) have lower completion rates, it signals those sections are too dense. Use quiz score data — if a module averages below 75%, the content may need to be simplified.
🏛
Assign a Human Onboarding Champion Per JK
The highest-completing JKs consistently have one named person responsible for follow-up. Automated reminders are supplementary — a personal check-in from an JK Lead converts more completions than any email.
📱
Mobile-First Design is Non-Negotiable
Most volunteers will complete this on a phone between prayer services. Ensure all modules render cleanly on mobile — long tables (like the infraction matrix) should scroll horizontally, not overflow the screen.
🔄
Annual Refresher — Not One-and-Done
Set the platform to prompt returning volunteers for an annual "refresher check" — a 5-question quiz covering policy updates. This keeps compliance current and surfaces anyone whose access has lapsed.
🧾
Tie Waiver Signing to Access — Hard Gate
Volunteers should not be able to start Module I until the Machform waiver is submitted. This prevents the scenario where someone completes all modules but never signs legal documentation. Build this as a prerequisite block, not a reminder.
📋
Separate S&S Compliance Tracking
Safety & Security volunteers have additional requirements (CPR, First Aid, Gatekeeper within 6 months). Track these separately in the reporting dashboard with their own compliance clock — they should not be grouped with general volunteer completion %.
🌐
Accessibility: Offer Language & Accessibility Options
If your Jamat includes non-English speakers or volunteers with visual impairments, plan for font-size controls, screen-reader compatibility, and at minimum one translated version of the critical CoC and Safety modules.
